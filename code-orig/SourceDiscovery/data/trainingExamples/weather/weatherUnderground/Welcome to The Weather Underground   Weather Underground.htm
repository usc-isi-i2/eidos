    Weather Summary - Tim Roche

    Weather Underground midday recap for Thursday, August 23, 2007.

    Heavy rain continued across flood stricken areas of the Mid-West on Thursday. To make matters worse some of the rain fell from intense thunderstorms with extremely high winds, hail and even tornadoes. Even temperatures were not cooperating with the flood relief efforts as searing temperatures reaching into 90s throughout some of the stricken areas. River levels in many locations remained well above major flood stage. In addition to flooding of rivers and small streams, urban flooding was problematic throughout the Mid-West on Thursday. Since rivers were high and the ground was already saturated, rain that fell over urbanized areas had no where to go, and remained on streets as storm sewers overflowed.

    Additional heavy rain was noted throughout the eastern interior on Thursday as strong thunderstorms developed throughout Pennsylvania and New York. Some of these storms to the west of Altoona, PA were dropping more than 2 inches of rain per hour.

    In the south, temperatures were quite warm, with the hottest locations experiencing triple digit temperatures, but relief was spread throughout the region in the form of afternoon thundershowers, which helped cool things down a bit. Temperatures were also hot in the West on Thursday as a ridge of high pressure in the eastern Pacific edged ashore a bit. While temperatures in the inland areas were warm, highs along the coast were much cooler as coastal fog returned to the region.

    Temperatures in the Lower 48 states Thursday have ranged from a morning low of 30 degrees at Stanley, Idaho to a midday high of 105 degrees at Huntsville, Ala.
